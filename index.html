The Problem: Clinical Risk & Outdated Technology

Microsuction Devices Have Remained Largely Unchanged Since the 1960s

In November 2024, ENT UK released updated clinical guidance on microsuction of the external ear canal.

ENT UK's 2024 clinical guidance identifies critical risks with legacy equipment:

  • Excessive Noise (up to 140 dB) — linked to patient distress, tinnitus, and potential hearing damage
  • Rigid Metal Tips — uncomfortable and potentially injurious in narrow or sensitive canals
  • Uncontrolled Suction — can trigger vertigo and caloric-induced inner ear trauma
  • Frequent Blockages — lead to delays, repeated tool changes, and longer procedure times
  • Clinician Fatigue — driven by acoustic load, poor ergonomics, and procedural inefficiency

These risks contribute to patient dissatisfaction, litigation exposure, and rising demand for safer alternatives.

Introducing Zephyr

Zephyr is a next-generation microsuction device engineered to solve the clinical, acoustic, and ergonomic challenges of traditional ear wax removal.

Developed by Spoke Medical in the UK, in close collaboration with ENT surgeons and audiologists, Zephyr combines precision suction with patient-centred safety.

Key Innovations:

  • Operates at ≤75 dB - dramatically reducing noise-induced complications
  • Complete silence when idle - improving clinic environment
  • Precision airflow control - enhancing clinician control and patient comfort
  • Anti-block technology - minimizing procedure interruptions
  • Ergonomic design - reducing clinician fatigue
  • Fully sterilized single-use packs - eliminating cross-infection risk
