The Problem: Clinical Risk & Outdated Technology

Microsuction devices have remained largely unchanged since the 1960s, presenting significant clinical risks identified by ENT UK's 2024 guidance.

Critical Risks with Legacy Equipment

  • Excessive Noise (up to 140 dB) — linked to patient distress, tinnitus, and potential hearing damage
  • Rigid Metal Tips — uncomfortable and potentially injurious in narrow or sensitive canals
  • Uncontrolled Suction — can trigger vertigo and caloric-induced inner ear trauma
  • Frequent Blockages — lead to delays, repeated tool changes, and longer procedure times
  • Clinician Fatigue — driven by acoustic load, poor ergonomics, and procedural inefficiency

These risks contribute to patient dissatisfaction, litigation exposure, and rising demand for safer alternatives.
